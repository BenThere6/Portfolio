About Me
Hello! I'm a passionate web developer with a unique journey into the world of coding. My adventure began in January 2020 at BYU-Idaho, where I first dabbled in programming. However, the onset of COVID-19 brought unexpected changes, leading me back to Utah and shifting my educational path. Though I completed my associate's degree in General Studies, my true passion lay in coding, a field I explored through numerous personal projects.
One of my proudest creations is the "Auto Budgeter," a Python-based tool that intelligently categorizes bank transactions and updates budgets in real-time. It's a testament to my love for developing efficient backend systems and solving real-life problems through technology.
Initially, web development didn't resonate with me. I was more fascinated by the logic and functionality behind the scenes than the aesthetics of front-end design. However, my desire to propel my career in technology led me to Rutgers Coding Bootcamp, a turning point where I discovered an unexpected appreciation for crafting visually appealing and user-friendly interfaces. The bootcamp's full-stack curriculum allowed me to deepen my backend skills and develop a newfound enjoyment in front-end development.
My latest project, the "Habit Tracker," showcases this integrated skill set. It's an application where users can track and manage their habits, seeing real-time progress and areas for improvement. This project embodies my ability to create comprehensive digital solutions from concept to execution.
Looking ahead, I'm excited to continue evolving the Habit Tracker with features like goal-setting, SMS notifications, and advanced data visualization. These enhancements aim to provide users with a more interactive and insightful experience.
Web development is more than a career choice for me; it's a passion that drives me to innovate and create. I'm eager to bring my blend of backend proficiency and newfound front-end skills to a professional setting, contributing to meaningful projects and growing further in this dynamic field.
